# Welcome, Spectator

*I wholeheartedly welcome you to witness the strange collection of things I create, break, rebuild, and occasionally understand.*

## How did I end up here?

Ah, now *that* is the interesting question.

You see, the unknown has never been enough for me. I do not simply wish to use things; I must know why they work, how they fail, and what happens when they are pushed far beyond what their creators intended.

## But enough of the *how* and *why*. Let us discuss the *where* and *when*.

I grew up in Hungary before moving to Vienna with my family during my teenage years. Ever since I became conscious, computers have fascinated me with their endless complexity and countless moving parts. Naturally, I responded in the healthiest possible way: by changing every setting imaginable and accidentally breaking my system several times.

Eventually, I discovered Visual Basic. At around twelve years old, I moved on to C#, WPF, and frontend web tinkering. Later, I spent quite some time working with Universal Windows Platform, where I drifted slightly into graphics programming with DirectX and C++/CX; Microsoft’s wonderfully cursed Frankenstein language that could never quite decide whether it wanted to be managed .NET or unmanaged C++.

These days, I am slowly migrating away from the ever-growing chaos of Windows, learning more about the Linux kernel while porting both private and community projects across platforms. Performance engineering is another long-standing interest of mine.

*(BUZZWORD ALERT!: Cache locality, memory behavior, branch prediction, and other wonderfully obsessive details. Side effects may include excessive benchmarking, staring at profilers for unhealthy amounts of time, and consulting your doctor, pharmacist, or local wizard.)*

Somewhere in the distance lies VR and AR development, along with all the wonderfully unhinged inside-out tracking algorithms that come with it. A dangerous place for sane individuals, certainly. But an interesting one nevertheless.

And honestly?

That is usually enough reason for me to continue.
